Accuracy and Efficiency

To cheaply create a prototype for this experiment, we can utilize open-source computer vision libraries (such as OpenCV) and existing sign language datasets to build a basic sign language recognition algorithm. Tools like TensorFlow and Keras can be used to train and test the algorithm on a small scale.

To involve potential customers, we can invite a diverse group of sign language users (varying in age, proficiency, and dialect) to participate in a controlled testing environment. Participants will be asked to perform a series of signed phrases, which the prototype will then attempt to translate in real-time.

Measurable outcomes:

1. The percentage of correctly translated signs and phrases.

2. The average time taken for the system to recognize and translate the signed input.
